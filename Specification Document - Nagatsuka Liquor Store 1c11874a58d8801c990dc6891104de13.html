Specification Document - Nagatsuka Liquor Store

Executive Summary

Nagatsuka Liquor Store is a family-owned business which as established in
1927. The business has built a strong reputation for its premium selection
of products. However, in the highly competitive market, the store faces
several challenges in customer engagement and brand visibility due to
lack of online communication.
The purpose of the project is to develop a professional website to brand
credibility, attract new customers and improve communication with
customers. The website will serve as a digital platform, addressing key
business objectives such as showcasing unique products.
A competitor analysis reveals that most liquor stores lack visually
appealing designs. The proposed website successfully prioritises a
modern, bold aesthetic which reflect brand values and personality.
Additionally advanced features such as search bar with filters and
language are included for business’ long-term development.
Although further refinement in details is required, by implementing this
website, Nagatsuka Liquor Store aims to establish a strong digital
presence, enhance customer relationships and secure its position as a
leader in the premium liquor retail sector in a long-term.

1. Application Purpose and Scope

a. Business background and market research

Nagatsuka Liquor Store was established in 1927 by Yoshisaburo Nagatsuka
and has been a family-operated business in Tokyo for nearly a century.
Over recent decades, competition in the liquor retail market has
intensified due to legal changes that permit supermarkets and
convenience stores to sell alcohol (Anchorman, 2024a). In response, the
store has specialized in premium alcoholic beverages, including sake and
wine, to differentiate itself.
Beyond competition, shifts in consumer behavior pose additional
challenges. Factors such as evolving taste preferences, economic
pressures, and Japan’s aging population have led to declining alcohol
consumption overall (Sakeportal, 2024a). To ensure long-term
sustainability, the business must establish competitive advantages
through branding, digital presence, and personalised experiences
(Anchorman, 2024b). One recent example is organising events in
collaboration with local restaurants, encouraging customers to taste new
flavours and fostering local community engagement.

Application Purpose and Scope

Despite its long-standing reputation, Nagatsuka Liquor Store lacks an official website, relying only on social media (Instagram and Facebook) for online communication. Having a website is crucial to enhancing brand credibility, promoting the brand, reaching new customers, and increasing in-store visits. According to Forbes (Council, 2020), a website is a fundamental business tool today for visibility, marketing, and customer interaction. The website aims to support the store’s long term sustainability in an highly competitive market by serving as a powerful communication platform

5 Prioritised objectives

Reflecting on the store’s current challenges and business
strategy, the website aims to achieve the following objectives:

  • Objective1 (5/5) - Display unique products

Nagatsuka Liquor Store offers unique products that not many stores carry, which is a highly competitive advantage compared to other stores. Promoting unique products can attract customers who seek premium products and new flavours. Although the website will not include an online shop due to current business operations, an online catalog can serve as a reference for in-store purchases.

  • Objective 2 (4/5) - Improve communication with customers

Currently, the only communication tools with customers are limited to Instagram, Facebook, in-person interactions, and phone calls. While Instagram and Facebook accounts are suitable for making information available quickly, the website is designed to serve other purposes. It will
include essential business information such as store details, contact methods, FAQs, and event announcements. This structured approach will improve customer interactions beyond social media.

  • Objective 3 (5/5) - Reach highly desirable market

As the overall market is shrinking, it is important to reach a highly desirable market—those who are most likely to visit the store and become repeat buyers. Having an online presence will help the business be exposed to a larger audience through SEO and digital marketing strategies.

  • Objective 4 (4/5) - Strengthen brand identity

With nearly 100 years of history, Nagatsuka Liquor Store has built brand trust and specialised product offerings. However, in a competitive market, providing additional value is crucial (Anchorman, 2024c). A well-designed
website can proactively enhance brand identity by delivering a cohesive message through compelling content and strategic visual elements.

  • Objective 5 (5/5) - Improve product discovery

To maintain a competitive advantage, it is crucial for the business to continuously introduce new offerings, and the website should reflect this innovation (Anchorman, 2024d). According to research, particularly for sake, about 40% of people find it challenging to pair sake with food, while more than 60% stated they want to try new flavours (楯の川酒造株式会社, 2022).
This indicates the need for professional guidance, which can be incorporated into the website through educational content, product filters, and a user-friendly search function. Even if customers do not purchase immediately, such features enhance brand engagement and product awareness in the long term.

3. Target Audience

Researches on consumer behaviour is conducted in order to identify target audience. According to research conducted by Daichishiko in 2019, 22% of Japanese respondents stated they do not consume alcohol. The research also revealed gender differences in drinking habits. Among men, 31% said they drink almost every day, while 22% reported drinking a few times a
week. For women, about half reported drinking between daily and a few times per month (CoLtd, n.d.). In terms of location, for both men and women, the most popular place to drink is at home—50% for men and 45% for women. Popular alcoholic beverages also differ by gender, with men
enjoying beer and women preferring cocktails. According to another study on consumption preferences, consumers are primarily influenced by taste, price, and product uniqueness when making purchasing decisions. Additionally, campaigns and recommendations significantly
impact their buying choices (店頭でのお酒購入についての意識調査, 2024).
Another study conducted by Datacom revealed some drinking preferences. The most popular reason to drink is to relax (28.2%), followed by enjoying alcohol with food (23.3%). Other reasons include stress relief (21.8%) and simply enjoying the taste (20.4%) (小林祐太, 2024).

4.1 小川商店　Koyama Liquor Store

Colour - 3.5/5

The colors of this website are
very simple. It uses white as the
background and dark-toned
colors, such as purple and blue,
as accent colors, which are
highlighted in buttons and
headlines. Light gray and subtle
shadows are also used to
separate sections.
The design is intended to create
a traditional Japanese
atmosphere and establish a
sense of trust and elegance by
using analogous colors of blue
and purple.

Font - 3/5

The website uses Yu Mincho, one
of the most commonly used
fonts. Mincho fonts have
decorative strokes at the end of
characters, similar to serif fonts
in English. This font is used to
create a traditional and elegant
impression in design (フォント日
本語・英語の種類・選び方と定番フ
ォントを紹介 | Adobe, n.d.a).
The headline and logo use a
bold font weight to create
hierarchy, which could be more
distinct if the font sizes were
larger for better design.

Contrast - 2/5

The website does not include much
contrast in design other than the
contrast between accent colors
and the white background, making
the design very simplistic and hard
to navigate effectively. This could
be improved by changing the size
or weight of fonts, using different
types of fonts, and incorporating
various shapes to create a visually
appealing design and establish a
clear hierarchy of information
(Contrast in Graphic Design:
Understanding What It Means and
How to Use It, n.d.).

Repetition/unity - 2.5/5

The website includes a number
of repetitive elements in
structure, colours, shapes, and
fonts, creating a comprehensive
design. The page design often
consists of rectangular shapes
with overlaid text and images.
In terms of colour, the same
rules are applied across pages.
For example, blue is used for
links and headings, while light
gray is used to separate
sections.

Alignment -3/5

On most pages, elements such as page
titles and body text are aligned to the
left. The landing page includes different
types of alignment—left, right, and
centre—guiding the user’s eye in a Z
pattern (20 Web Design Principles to
Follow, n.d.). On desktop, elements are
distributed across the page, while on
mobile screens, they are consistently
aligned to the left, improving
readability.
On the product page, the table
categories and item alignments are
inconsistent, which could be improved
to create a more structured and visually
cohesive layout.

Proximity - 5/5

Information is clearly
categorized into sections and
topics by incorporating
rectangular elements, making it
easy for the user to understand.
On the landing page, each
content block includes different
designs and padding, improving
the layout.

Input Interface - 3/5

The website is designed to be
user-friendly—navigation bars,
dropdown menus, scrollable
pages, and clickable buttons are
simple and easy to understand.
The search bar allows users to
search for products by name,
location, and producer.
However, this feature may not
be effective if the user is not
familiar with alcohol. It could be
improved by providing
suggestions rather than giving
users full control.

Output Interface - 3/5

The loading speed can be slow, although
the website provides visual feedback
with an animated logo. Each page
includes animations where images and
text slide in after loading, which can be
slightly distracting to the user. The
website has a responsive design that
works on both desktop and mobile.
However, on the landing page, when
scrolling down, the size of the automated
video changes, causing a shaking effect
that disrupts the experience.

4.4 Recommendation

Based on the competitor analysis, the website for Nagatsuka Liquor Store will be designed to address key
insights observed in competing sites while ensuring a user-centric experience. Most competitor websites
follow a simplistic structure but lack strong design elements to reinforce brand identity. Additionally, they rely
heavily on text with minimal visual elements, despite the importance of a clear visual hierarchy in modern web
design, which enhances communication effectiveness (20 Web Design Principles to Follow, n.d.).
Furthermore, many competitor websites fail to utilise images effectively, often featuring low-quality visuals
that disrupt the user experience.
To differentiate itself, the proposed website will incorporate strong branding through the strategic use of
colors, shapes, and visual elements. It will also feature recommended products to enhance user engagement
and highlight the store’s unique offerings. Additionally, the website will prioritise high-quality visuals to create
an immersive experience and improve product discovery.

6.0 Proposed Application

6.1 Main features

The website will allow users to search for products using a variety of
categories. Additionally, registered users can save products as a
reference when purchasing in-store. This feature enables the business to
better understand user preferences, leading to a more personalised
shopping experience. This sets the business apart from supermarkets
and convenience stores. The website will highlight the store’s unique selling points, such as events, special products, and exclusive offerings. Promotions and announcements will be displayed at the top of the homepage to instantly capture users' attention. A blog section will keep users engaged with the brand while educating those interested in alcohol, helping them expand their knowledge. The website design aims to be bold and modern while maintaining trustworthiness, making it relevant to the current audience. Additionally, as Japan’s domestic market continues to shrink (Sakeportal, 2024a), the website will also be designed to appeal to non-Japanese customers by incorporating an internationally friendly design and language options.
