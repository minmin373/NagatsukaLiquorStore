Our Story

Since 1927, we've been serving our community with the finest Japanese liquor

Our History

1927

Beginnings

In 1927, Yoshisaburo Nagatsuka and his wife Shizu began their business by selling food, firewood, and goods from a cart in the Tanaka-cho area. After being advised by a fortune teller, Yoshisaburo shifted to selling alcohol, though the business was paused during WWII due to rationing.

Post-War

Post-War Revival

After the war, the store resumed its door-to-door sales. Yoshisaburo became known for his punctuality and trustworthiness, with customers relying on him to deliver their orders and handle payments accurately.

1953

Expansion and Challenges

In 1953, the store moved to a more strategic location and expanded its offerings. Yoshisaburo's son, Kiyoshi, took over in 1974, but the business faced difficulties in the 1990s with the rise of competition and the need for updates to the aging store.

1998

Modernization and Growth

In 1998, Kiyoshi's son, Shinichi, revitalized the store by redesigning it into a specialized space, taking advantage of the wine boom and setting new sales records. The store shifted its focus to providing unique experiences, hosting events like the "Nagatsuka Bar" and "Nagatsuka Marche" to engage customers and foster loyalty.
